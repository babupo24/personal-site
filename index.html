About Me

I am a computational physicist and data scientist with a PhD in Physics from Temple University. My work sits at the intersection of Monte Carlo simulations, medical imaging, and machine learning, with a strong focus on radiotherapy applications.

Over the last several years I have worked on charged-particle transport modeling, detector simulations, auto-contouring for MR-only workflows, and synthetic CT generation. I enjoy building robust software pipelines that connect physical models, numerical methods, and practical clinical workflows.

I also have extensive teaching experience in physics and mathematics, and I enjoy explaining complex ideas in clear, structured ways – whether in the classroom, documentation, or code.

Research & Selected Projects

Monte Carlo · Medical Physics

Monte Carlo Modeling for MR-Guided Radiotherapy

Development and optimization of Monte Carlo simulation modules for dose calculation and beam modeling in MR-guided radiotherapy systems. Work includes charged-particle transport, multiple scattering models, and performance tuning on multi-core and HPC platforms.

ML · Auto-contouring

Kidney Autocontouring & Adaptive SVM Models

Designed and analyzed SVM-based and multi-kernel methods for kidney autocontouring on MR images, including edge-aware filtering, diffusion-based regularization, and probabilistic modeling of segmentation outputs.

Synthetic CT · MR-only

Synthetic CT Generation for MR-Only Workflows

Worked on converting MR image volumes and structure sets into synthetic CT representations for radiotherapy planning. Involved DICOM processing, image registration, feature engineering, and supervised learning for HU prediction.

Collider Physics

Dihadron Correlations at STAR

Contributed to the analysis of unpolarized dihadron cross-sections and azimuthal correlation asymmetries in proton-proton collisions at √s = 200 GeV within the STAR Collaboration. Work included detector calibration, large-scale data processing, and statistical analysis of hadronic observables.
